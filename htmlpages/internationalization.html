Overview: Internationalization

What is Internationalization?

Internationalization means preparing a shop system so that it can be used in multiple countries, languages, and markets. It ensures that the technical foundation supports multilingual content, different currencies, local laws, and cultural differences.

Multilingual Capabilities

A system must support different languages for:

  • Product descriptions
  • User interface elements
  • Emails and order confirmations

Multiple Currencies

International shops should support:

  • Dynamic or manual currency conversion
  • Different rounding rules
  • Accurate display of local prices

Localization & Regional Formats

Regional differences affect:

  • Date formats (e.g., DD.MM.YYYY vs. MM/DD/YYYY)
  • Decimal and thousand separators
  • Measurement units (cm, inch, kg, lb)

Legal and Tax Requirements

Each country has its own legal rules. Shops need adjustable settings for:

  • Different VAT rates
  • EU-specific rules (e.g., reverse charge)
  • Privacy requirements such as GDPR

Payment & Shipping for Multiple Countries

International customers expect familiar service options:

  • Country-specific payment methods
  • Different shipping zones
  • Worldwide delivery options

SEO for International Markets

To rank globally, shops need:

  • hreflang tags
  • Localized URLs
  • Translated metadata
