Payment Methods in Online Shops

Payment methods are a crucial part of every online shop because they directly influence the conversion rate. Customers expect their preferred payment option to be available — otherwise many abandon the checkout process. Modern shops integrate multiple payment providers to stay flexible and competitive.

Common payment gateways include PayPal, Stripe, Klarna, Mollie, and Amazon Pay. Depending on the target audience and the country, different methods may perform better or worse.

Popular Payment Methods

  • Credit & Debit Cards: Visa, Mastercard, American Express — widely used and fast.
  • PayPal: very popular due to buyer protection and convenience.
  • Klarna / Buy Now Pay Later: allows customers to pay later or in installments.
  • Bank Transfer (SEPA): secure and especially common in Europe.
  • Digital Wallets: Apple Pay, Google Pay — fast mobile checkout.
  • Local Methods: Sofortüberweisung, iDEAL, EPS, Bancontact depending on region.

Cost Factors

  • Transaction fees: percentage + fixed fee per order.
  • Currency conversion fees: relevant for international customers.
  • Chargeback fees: when customers request refunds via their bank.
  • Monthly gateway fees: some providers charge extra for premium features.

Security & Compliance

  • 3D Secure 2: additional verification for credit card payments.
  • PCI‑DSS: security standard for handling card data.
  • Fraud protection: automatic risk checks and monitoring.
