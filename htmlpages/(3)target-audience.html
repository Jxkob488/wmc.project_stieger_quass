The target audience describes the group of people who are most likely to buy products in an online shop. It includes demographic, social, technical and behavioral factors.

A clear understanding of the target audience helps businesses design their shop, choose suitable marketing strategies and improve customer satisfaction.

üìä Demographic Characteristics

Demographic factors describe basic information about customers such as age, gender, education, income and family status.

These characteristics influence buying decisions. For example, younger customers often prefer trendy products, while older customers may focus more on quality and reliability.

üß† Psychological and Behavioral Factors

Psychological factors include interests, values, lifestyle and personal preferences. They strongly influence how customers perceive a shop.

Behavioral factors describe how customers shop online. Examples are how often they buy, how much they spend and how long they compare products.

Business customers purchase products for professional use. Examples include office equipment, software and industrial products.

Customers from different regions have different expectations regarding language, design and payment methods.

The customer journey describes the steps from first contact to purchase. These include information search, comparison and checkout.
