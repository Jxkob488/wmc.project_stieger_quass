Overview: Cost Structure

Online shop systems follow different cost models depending on technology, hosting approach, and the target business size. The most common approach is subscription-based pricing, where the platform is offered as a cloud service (SaaS) with monthly or yearly plans. These subscriptions often include hosting, updates, security, and basic support.

Self-hosted platforms such as WooCommerce or PrestaShop use a more flexible and modular cost structure. The base system is often free, but additional expenses arise from hosting, premium themes, plugins, and maintenance. This model can be cheaper at the start but more complex in the long run.

Enterprise systems (e.g., Adobe Commerce, SAP Commerce) rely on annual or multi‑year licensing fees. They provide advanced scalability, integrations, automation tools, and professional support — but they require a significantly larger budget.

Besides the base pricing, transaction fees are an important cost factor. Payment providers like PayPal, Stripe, or Klarna charge a percentage of each order plus a fixed fee. Some platforms add their own transaction fees if you don’t use their preferred payment gateway.

Typical Cost Categories

  • Platform or license fees: monthly subscriptions (SaaS) or yearly enterprise licenses.
  • Hosting costs: from basic shared hosting to scalable cloud environments.
  • Theme and design costs: one-time theme purchases or premium templates.
  • Plugins and extensions: added functionality, integrations, analytics, SEO tools.
  • Development and customization: adapting the shop to business needs, building custom features.
  • Maintenance and security updates: especially relevant for self-hosted platforms.
  • Marketing tools and services: email tools, ads, automation, tracking, customer loyalty systems.
  • Transaction fees: per‑order charges from payment providers; varies by country and volume.

Example Price Ranges

  • SaaS platforms: €20–€300 per month depending on features.
  • Self-hosted systems: €5–€40 per month for hosting, plus paid plugins (€20–€200 each).
  • Enterprise solutions: €15,000–€200,000+ per year including support and scalability.
  • Development costs: €500–€10,000+ depending on customization level.
