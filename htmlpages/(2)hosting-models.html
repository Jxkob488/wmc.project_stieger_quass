Hosting Models

Hosting models describe how and where an online shop is operated. They define who manages servers, software updates, security and scalability. Choosing the right hosting model is an important strategic decision that affects both costs and flexibility.

In e-commerce, three main hosting models are commonly used: Software as a Service (SaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS).

SaaS – Software as a Service

SaaS solutions provide a complete shop system that is hosted and maintained by the provider. Users access the platform via a web browser and pay a recurring subscription fee.

This model is ideal for small businesses and beginners because it requires no technical knowledge and allows fast setup.

  • No server management required
  • Fast setup
  • Predictable monthly costs
  • Limited customization

PaaS – Platform as a Service

PaaS provides a platform for developers to build and deploy applications without managing physical servers. The provider handles infrastructure, while developers focus on functionality.

This model offers more flexibility than SaaS but still simplifies server management.

  • Developer-friendly environment
  • Scalable infrastructure
  • More control than SaaS
  • Platform dependency

IaaS – Infrastructure as a Service

IaaS offers full control over servers, storage and networking. Businesses manage the entire software stack themselves.

This model is suitable for large companies with experienced technical teams and specific requirements.

  • Maximum flexibility
  • High scalability
  • High technical complexity
  • Greater responsibility

Comparison of Hosting Models

Each hosting model serves a different purpose. SaaS focuses on simplicity, PaaS balances flexibility and convenience, while IaaS offers full control at the cost of complexity.

Choosing the Right Hosting Model

The best hosting model depends on business size, technical skills and long-term goals. It is important to consider future growth and maintenance effort before making a decision.
