The cost structure of an online shop includes all expenses related to setup, operation and long-term maintenance. These costs depend on the chosen shop system, hosting model and business size.

A clear understanding of costs helps avoid budget problems and allows better planning as the shop grows.

üì¶ Platform and License Costs

Some shop systems charge monthly or yearly fees for using their platform. These costs may vary depending on features, sales volume or number of users.

Open-source systems usually have no license fees but may require higher spending on setup and technical maintenance.

üñ•Ô∏è Hosting Costs

Hosting costs depend on performance requirements and traffic volume. Simple hosting is inexpensive, while scalable cloud solutions cost more.

As traffic increases, upgrading the hosting environment becomes necessary to ensure reliability.

üé® Design and Theme Costs

Design affects user experience and brand perception. Many systems offer free themes, while premium themes or custom designs increase costs.

Custom designs provide flexibility but are usually more expensive.

üß© Plugins and Extensions

Plugins add features such as payment methods, analytics or marketing tools. Some are free, while others require one-time or recurring payments.

Overusing paid plugins can significantly increase monthly costs.

üîß Maintenance and Support

Regular updates, backups and security checks are essential for a stable shop.

Maintenance can be handled internally or by external service providers, which affects overall costs.

üìä Summary

A realistic cost structure includes both fixed and variable expenses.

Careful planning ensures that the shop remains profitable and scalable.
